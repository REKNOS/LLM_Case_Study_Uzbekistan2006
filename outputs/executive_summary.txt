Here is the analysis as a UN development analyst:

**Executive Summary (150 words maximum)**

Uzbekistan's health care system has undergone significant reforms since 2004, aiming to make it more accessible and equitable for all population groups. The government has implemented measures to improve rural health care services, including the creation of new health care institutions in rural areas staffed by qualified health care professionals. The country aims to provide unlimited access to primary health care, which is currently free-of-charge, even in a market economy. The system also focuses on preventive health care and improving the quality of health care services. However, despite these efforts, challenges persist, including limited resources and inadequate infrastructure.

**Five Key Findings**

1. Uzbekistan's health care system has made significant progress in increasing access to primary health care, particularly for rural populations.
2. The government's efforts to improve rural health care services have led to an increase in the number of health care institutions in rural areas.
3. There is still a shortage of qualified health care professionals in rural areas, which hampers the quality of health care services.
4. The system faces significant challenges in terms of limited resources and inadequate infrastructure, particularly in rural areas.
5. Preventive health care and improving the quality of health care services remain key priorities for the government.

**Five Key Policy Priorities**

1. **Increase investment in rural health care infrastructure**: Upgrade existing facilities and build new ones to ensure that they meet international standards.
2. **Recruit and retain more qualified health care professionals**: Implement incentives and training programs to attract and retain qualified health care staff, particularly in rural areas.
3. **Strengthen primary health care services**: Expand the scope of primary health care services to include a wider range of health services, including preventive care.
4. **Improve access to essential medicines**: Ensure that all population groups have access to affordable and quality essential medicines.
5. **Enhance community engagement and participation**: Increase community involvement in health care decision-making processes and promote health literacy among the public.
